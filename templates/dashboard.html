Master the Art of Spotting Phishing

Phishing attacks are the #1 way companies are breached. This interactive quiz will teach you the red flags to look for and the best practices to keep your accounts secure.

🚩 Key Phishing Red Flags

  • ⏰ Urgency/Threats – "Act now or lose access"
  • 📧 Mismatched domain – amaz0n.com vs amazon.com
  • 🔗 Suspicious links – hover to reveal real URL
  • 🔐 MFA pressure – emails demanding re-auth
  • 📎 .exe attachments – never open executables
  • 💷 Unexpected invoices – verify via another channel
  • 👤 Generic greetings – "Dear Customer"
  • 🖊️ Grammar errors – typos and odd phrasing

🔒 MFA Phishing – What to Watch For

Multi-Factor Authentication phishing is one of the fastest growing attack vectors. Attackers create fake pages that relay your OTP in real-time ("Adversary-in-the-Middle").

  • 🔴 Emails claiming your MFA token has expired or needs resetting
  • 🔴 Links that look like your company's SSO or login portal
  • 🔴 Any message demanding your one-time code or push-approval
  • 🟢 Safe action: Always navigate to the login portal directly – never via email links

⚖️ Legal & Ethical Compliance Analysis

PhishSim v7.0 — Internal Security Awareness Platform  |  Prepared: March 2026

1. Scope & Purpose

This document outlines the legal and ethical framework governing the use of PhishSim, an automated phishing simulation and security-awareness training platform deployed exclusively within authorised organisational boundaries.

PhishSim is designed to:

  • 📧 Simulate realistic phishing emails to measure and improve employee cyber-resilience
  • 📚 Deliver mandatory and voluntary security-awareness training to all staff
  • 📊 Provide management with actionable, anonymised risk metrics
  • 🔁 Automate escalation of training for high-risk or repeat-offending users
⚠️ Authorised Use Only. This platform must only be operated by approved administrators within an organisation that has granted explicit, documented management consent. Any unapproved or external use is strictly prohibited and may constitute a criminal offence.

2. Legal Framework

2.1 UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) & Data Protection Act 2018

The processing of employee data within PhishSim is conducted under Article 6(1)(f) – Legitimate Interests of the UK GDPR, specifically the organisation's legitimate interest in protecting its IT systems and data from real phishing attacks. The following obligations apply:

PrincipleHow PhishSim Complies
Lawfulness, Fairness & TransparencyStaff are informed of the simulation programme via the acceptable-use policy. No deception beyond the scope of the simulation itself.
Purpose LimitationData collected is used solely for security training and risk assessment — never for performance management or disciplinary processes without separate HR process.
Data MinimisationOnly click events, training completions, and risk scores are stored. No real credentials, message content, or sensitive personal data are captured.
Storage LimitationSimulation records are retained for a maximum of 12 months, then purged from the database.
Integrity & ConfidentialityAll data is stored locally (SQLite/Firebase). Access is restricted to authorised admins with role-based authentication.

2.2 Computer Misuse Act 1990 (UK)

Phishing simulations could, if misdirected, fall under Section 1 (Unauthorised Access) or Section 3 (Unauthorised Acts) of the Computer Misuse Act 1990. PhishSim mitigates this through:

  • ✅ Written authorisation from senior management before any simulation is launched
  • ✅ Simulated emails are sent only to registered internal users within the platform
  • ✅ No real phishing infrastructure is deployed — all landing pages are internal and educational
  • ✅ No real credentials are harvested, stored, or transmitted

2.3 ICO Guidance on Employee Monitoring

The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) requires that employee monitoring be necessary, proportionate, and communicated. PhishSim complies by:

  • ✅ Limiting tracking to the click of a simulated phishing link (binary event, not content)
  • ✅ Including simulation programme details in the organisation's Privacy Notice and Acceptable Use Policy
  • ✅ Not using results as grounds for disciplinary action without a separate HR procedure

2.4 Employment Law Considerations

Under the Employment Rights Act 1996 and common law duty of mutual trust, employees must not be subjected to covert, unfair, or unduly stressful monitoring. PhishSim addresses this by:

  • ✅ Ensuring simulations are proportionate and not designed to humiliate
  • ✅ Providing immediate, supportive training feedback (not punitive messaging) when a link is clicked
  • ✅ Ensuring results feed into learning objectives, not performance ratings

3. Ethical Principles

🔍

Transparency

All employees are made aware that phishing simulations may occur as part of the organisation's security programme. The existence of the programme — though not individual simulation dates — is disclosed in advance.

⚖️

Proportionality

Simulation difficulty is calibrated to employee risk level. High-risk users receive harder simulations, but volume and intensity remain proportionate to organisational need.

🚫

No Entrapment

Simulations are educational, not punitive. The goal is behaviour change through awareness — not to "catch out" employees. Results are used to tailor training, not to discipline.

🔒

Data Minimisation

Only the minimum data necessary is recorded: whether a user clicked a simulated link, and whether they completed training. No keystroke logging, email content capture, or real credential collection occurs.

🤝

Respect & Dignity

Simulated phishing emails avoid themes that could cause distress (e.g., bereavement, medical emergencies). All training feedback is constructive, supportive, and non-judgmental.

🌱

Continuous Improvement

The platform tracks behaviour change over time. The goal is measurable, sustained improvement in security resilience — not one-off compliance exercises.
