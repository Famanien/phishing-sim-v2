Phishing Simulation – You Were Caught!

This was a safety test from your organisation's security team. No real credentials were captured.

In a real attack, the credentials you just entered would now be in the hands of an attacker. Here's what should have given this page away:

  • 🔗
    Suspicious URL — The address bar showed 127.0.0.1:5000/login instead of your company's real domain.
  • 📧
    You arrived via a link — Legitimate sign-in pages are accessed by navigating directly, not from an email link.
  • ⏰
    Urgency or pressure — The original email likely created a sense of urgency to make you act without thinking.
  • 🔏
    No MFA prompt appeared — Real corporate portals typically require multi-factor authentication.
💡
What to do next time: Always check the URL before entering credentials. If you clicked a link in an email, navigate to the company portal directly in a fresh browser tab instead.
